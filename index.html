About

English

Hello, My name is Nathan Brock, and this is my Ojibwe website. I'm from Champlin, Minnesota, and I made this website to provide a free online resource that gives a comprehensive overview on one of the largest indigenous languages north of Mexico. Despite having a background in linguistics, I want this site to be a resource that anybody can understand regardless of their education level. This site will mention various sources that the user may visit to gain further understanding into various aspects of the Ojibwe language mentioned here. There will also be some resources mentioned that learners may wish to pursue.

DISCLAIMER: I am not a native speaker. As I learn more about the language I will continue to update this site, but if a native speaker tells you something different, listen to them. For native speakers, if you see something on here that is inaccurate, please let me know.

Anishinaabemowin

Aaniin, Nathan Brock indezhinikaaz, o'ow dash nindanishinaabekwaawin . Champlin, Minnesota indoonjbaa.

Navigation

If this is your first time learning the Ojibwe language, I recommend that you learn the following topics in this order due to their progression in difficulty:

  • Pronunciation & Alphabet
  • Pronouns
  • Nouns
  • Intransitive Verbs
  • Transitive Verbs

Each page will have an interactive activity that the user can use to expand their vocabulary and practice the concepts that have been presented in that lesson.

About the Ojibwe Language

Ojibwe is an Algonquin language with speakers living in Minnesota, Wisconsin, North Dakota, and many parts of Canada.
