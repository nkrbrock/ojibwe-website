Pronouns

Once you know how to pronounce Ojibwe, the next important step is to learn how the language talks about person. Understanding how person functions is important to form both nouns and verbs. In English we have 7 different pronouns:

I 1st person singular
we 1st person plural
you 2nd person singular and plural
he masculine 3rd person plural
she femenine 3rd person plural
it neutral 3rd person plural
they 3rd person plural

Ojibwe has a slightly more complicated pronoun system:

Niin 1st person singular
Niinawind 1st person plural exclusive
Giinawind 1st person plural inclusive
Giin 2nd person singular
Giinawaa 2nd person plural
Wiin 3rd person singular animate
Wiinawaa 3rd person plural animate

1st person plural inclusive would be similar to saying "we" in English and that would include yourself whereas 1st person plural exclusive is "we" but it does not include you. Animate and inanimate are the grammatical cases that are used in Ojibwe similar to Spanish masculine and femenine. In addition to these 7 persons specified by pronouns, there are also additional roles that a noun can take. These are:

indefinite or unspecified equivalent to "someone does ___"
3rd person animate obviative/4th person when 2+ 3rd person or named nouns are present, the less important one is marked for this
3rd person plural inanimate obviative when 2+ 3rd person or named nouns are present, the less important one is marked for this
3rd person inanimate obviative/4th person when 2+ 3rd person or named nouns are present, the less important one is marked for this
3rd person singular inanimate similar to "it" in English or the nouns that would take that place
3rd person plural inanimate similar to "those things" in English or the nouns that would take that place
3rd person animate possessed by obviative

The reason why pronouns are being discussed before either nouns or pronouns is because pronouns are used to modify both of these parts of speech. When indicating possession for something or conjugating intransitive verbs, both the item being possessed and the noun take on the following prefixes (note that 3rd person prefix only goes with nouns).

first letter first person (n) second person (g) third person (w)
o nindo- gido- odo-
a aa e i nind- gid- od-
oo n- g- od-
ii n- g- w-
b nim- gi- o-
g d ' j z zh nin- gi- o-
p t k h ch m n s sh w y ni- gi- o-

It is important to note that depending on the specific dialect, there may be some omissions or changes for this chart, so if you are learning a particular dialect it is important to prioritize that over these charts.
